Autonomic Dysreflexia
Definition and Cause

Autonomic Dysreflexia (AD) is a reaction to something that causes pain or discomfort to the body below the level of injury in people who are injured at T6 or higher. It does not occur in everyone.

Autonomic Dysreflexia is a medical emergency and should be recognized and addressed quickly

  • Seen in injuries at T6 and higher
  • More likely in complete injuries
  • Is caused by irritation to the body where there is no feeling-below the level of injury

Common causes include:

  • Overfull bladder
  • Overfull bowel
  • Pain below the level of injury
  • Skin problem such as a burn, too much pressure, blister, or too cold
  • Tight clothes
  • Ingrown toenail
  • Sexual intercourse or ejaculation
  • Bladder infection
  • Menstrual cramp
  • Labor and contractions
Features
  • Sweating and flushing above the level of injury
  • Goosebumps above the level of injury
  • Dry and clammy below the level of injury
  • Pounding headache
  • Rise in systolic blood pressure (top number in a blood pressure reading) 20 units higher than usual
  • Stuffy nose
  • Blurry vision or seeing spots
  • Slow heartbeat
Treatment

Discuss with your doctor the best way to recognize and address symptoms of autonomic dysreflexia

Some common methods to address the most common causes of AD are:

  • Sit up
  • Loosen clothing
  • Check bladder and catheterize to make sure bladder is empty
  • Search for other causes if symptoms persist
  • Check to make sure bowel is not full by performing bowel routine

Seek medical attention if symptoms persist despite addressing common causes of AD

Medical providers will conduct a thorough exam and may give blood pressure lowering medication
