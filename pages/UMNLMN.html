Upper Motor Neuron vs Lower Motor Neuron

In order to move, the brain sends messages through nerves called Upper Motor Neurons (UMN) that travel down the spinal cord. These nerves communicate to other nerves that exit the spinal cord called Lower Motor Neurons (LMN), carrying the message to muscles to move.

Upper Motor Neuron

Injuries to the spinal cord (above level L1 when the cord ends and the cauda equina begins) are called Upper Motor Neuron injuries. In these injuries, the connection from the brain to the muscles is broken and the muscles under the level of the injury are either partially or fully paralyzed.

Lower Motor Neuron

Injuries below the spinal cord that affect the cauda equina or conus medullaris are called Lower Motor Neuron injuries. Muscles below the level of injury are also either partially or fully paralyze, and are loose with inactive reflexes are not active.
