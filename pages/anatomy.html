What does the spinal cord look like?

The spinal cord is a tight collection of nerves that runs from the brain down to the low back. It is protected by 29 backbones (vertebrae) that make up the spine

The vertebrae are labeled into 4 groups based on location:

Group Area of body Number of vertebrae
Cervical Neck 7
Thoracic Chest 12
Lumbar Low Back 5
Sacral Low Back to tailbone 5 (fused into 1)

Vertebrae are numbered by group and number counting from the top of the spine to the bottom

The spinal cord ends at about the L1 level at a structure called the conus medullaris. Then there are nerves that continue down in a looser arrangement called the cauda equina, in latin meaning "horse's tail"

From each verteba, spinal nerves exit the spinal cord or cauda equina to send and receive messages to and from the body

The first nerve to exit the spinal cord is called C1, C for cervical. Then C2, C3 and so on until C8

  • Cervical: C1-8
  • Thoracic: T1-12
  • Lumbar: L1-5
  • Sacral: S1-5

The last nerves at the bottom of the spinal cord are S4-5.
