What is the Spine?

The spine is a long collection of bones that runs from the neck down to the tailbone. It is made up of 33 backbones (vertebrae)

The vertebrae are labeled into 5 groups and are numbered counting from the top of the spine to the bottom:

Group Area of body Number of vertebrae
Cervical Neck 7
Thoracic Chest 12
Lumbar Low Back 5
Sacral Low Back to tailbone 5 (fused into 1)
Coccygeal End of tailbone 4 (fused into 1)
What is the Spinal Cord?

The spinal cord is a tight collection of nerves that runs from the brain down to the low back. The spinal cord relays signals from the brain to the body, skin, muscles, and organs, and receives messages back send to the brain.

The spinal cord ends at about the L1 level at a structure called the conus medullaris. Then there are nerves that continue down in a looser arrangement called the cauda equina, in latin meaning "horse's tail"

Spinal nerves at each vertebral level exit the spinal cord to send and receive messages to and from the body

The first nerve to exit the spinal cord is called C1, C for cervical. Then C2, C3 and so on until C8

  • Cervical: C1-C8
  • Thoracic: T1-T12
  • Lumbar: L1-L5
  • Sacral: S1-S5

The last nerves at the bottom of the spinal cord are S4-5.
