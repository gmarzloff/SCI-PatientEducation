Bladder Function
  • Urine is made in the kidneys, then travels down to the bladder in 2 tubes called ureters
  • The bladder stores and then releases urine when ready
  • The bladder is made of a muscular wall that stretches and fills slowly with urine during the day as we drink and make urine
  • As the muscle of the bladder stretches, signals are sent to the brain so we can feel that we have to empty our bladder
  • Then, if it's the right time and appropriate to go, signals are sent back to the bladder to contract or squeeze the bladder muscle
  • The urine leaves the bladder through a small ring of muscle called a sphincter which is a gate-keeper: it stays tight to keep urine in, and relaxes to allow urine out
  • In order to get all the urine out of the bladder, the bladder muscle must contract and the sphincter must relax
  • The signals to coordinate these two actions are disrupted in SCI in different ways based on if the injury is Upper Motor Neuron (UMN) or Lower Motor Neuron (LMN)
  • The goal for management is to stay dry, empty the bladder, and prevent problems like infections or kidney damage
