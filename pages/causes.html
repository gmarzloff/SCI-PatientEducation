There are traumatic and nontraumatic causes of spinal cord injury

Traumatic causes are most commonly motor vehicles collisions and falls. See the link below for the most current statistics published in the United States

Nontraumatic causes include:

  • Vascular (spinal stroke, similar to a stroke in the brain)
  • Tumors growing on or near the spinal cord that can be cancerous or noncancerous (benign)
  • Infections or inflammation (irritation to the spinal cord)
  • Degenerative (advanced arthritis in the joints near the spinal cord that puts pressure on the spinal cord)
  • Other medical syndromes
    • Multiple Sclerosis
    • Transverse Myelitis
    • Spina Bifida
    • Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis (ALS)
    • Others
