Complete and Incomplete Injuries
Complete Injury

An injury is considered "complete" if the very bottom of the spinal cord does not receive any motor signals from the brain (no movement) or send any signals up from the body to the brain (no sensation or feeling). The bottom of the spinal cord is level S4-5 and controls the motor activity and sensation at the anus.

Incomplete Injury

An "incomplete" SCI is any injury to the spinal cord that still allows some sensory or motor function at the bottom of the spinal cord, S4-5. There is a large variation in the amount of signals that can be sent through a spinal cord injury. Strength and sensation below a SCI can vary from not present to almost fully intact
