Outcomes
What to expect after a SCI

Spinal cord injuries at different levels lead to different outcomes based on the muscles affected

The level of completeness also impacts outcome. This section will be divided by level of injury and will assume a complete injury. Injuries that are incomplete may have different outcomes.

Please discuss with a healthcare provider any questions about expected outcomes

Outcomes listed below include:

  • Movement: What actions can be done with muscles that receive intact signals from the brain
  • Activities of Daily Living: At certain levels of injury, individuals move from requiring assistance, to becoming independent in these tasks, for example:
    • Getting dressed
    • Brushing teeth
    • Brushing hair
    • Transfers
    • Feeding oneself
  • Elimination: Bowel and Bladder needs
  • Mobility: How an individual can move around
Functional Expectations by Level of Injury
  • C1-C3

    Movement: Patial movement of head and neck
    Activities of Daily Living: With assistance
    Elimination: With assistance
    Mobility: Power Wheelchair controlled by Sip-n-puff, head array, chin control, or eye gaze.

  • C4

    Movement: Full movement of Head and neck
    Activities of Daily Living: With assistance
    Elimination: With assistance
    Mobility: Power Wheelchair controlled by head array or chin control

  • C5

    Movement: Controls head, neck and shoulders, bends elbows
    Activities of Daily Living: With assistance
    Elimination: With assistance
    Mobility: Power Wheelchair with goal-post joystick controller

  • C6

    Movement: More shoulder movement, extend wrist to lift hand up
    Activities of Daily Living: Independent or with some assistance
    Elimination: Possible independently after set-up with appropriate equipment for men, for women would require a procedure
    Mobility: Power Wheelchair

  • C7-C8

    Movement: Straighten arm, bend wrist, some use of fingers
    Activities of Daily Living: Independent
    Elimination: Independent
    Mobility: Manual wheelchair

  • T1-T5

    Movement: Full hand and finger control, posture muscles in the upper chest are active, but not lower, so sitting balance is impaired
    Activities of Daily Living: Independent
    Elimination: Independent
    Mobility: Manual wheelchair

  • T6-T10

    Movement: Abdominal muscles are active so sitting balance is improved
    Activities of Daily Living: Independent
    Elimination: Independent
    Mobility: Manual wheelchair

  • T11-L5

    Movement: Bend and kick out the leg from the hip, bend and straighten the knees
    Activities of Daily Living: Independent
    Elimination: Independent
    Mobility: Manual Wheelchair, some may be able to walk short distances or longer with braces and assistive devices like a cane or walker

  • S1-S5

    Movement: Full control of legs, partial to full control of ankles
    Activities of Daily Living: Independent
    Elimination: Independent
    Mobility: Walks with support such as leg braces or canes
