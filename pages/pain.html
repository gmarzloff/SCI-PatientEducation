Pain after SCI
Direct Injury Pain

Any pain from a cut, bruise, broken bone, or pulled muscle for example, all caused by a direct injury. This type of pain should go away when the body heals.

  • How it feels: Sharp, deep, cutting, throbbing
  • Examples: After a surgery, breaking a bone, a papercut
Overuse Injury Pain

When using muscles and joints over and over again, they can become painful. This can happen gradually over time and may require activity modification - changing the way of doing an activity to not overuse those muscles and joints.

  • How it feels: Achy, throbbing, dull, sore
  • Examples: Using arms to wheel a wheelchair, using shoulder to throw a baseball
Neuropathic (Nerve) Pain

After spinal cord injury, the nerves of the spinal cord at times send faulty or incomplete signals to the brain, and the brain interprets them as discomfort or pain. This type of pain may not develop right away, and it may resolve with time. There are specific medications to help control this pain so please discuss with a medical provider.

  • How it feels: Burning, tingling, electric, sharp, pins and needs, shooting
  • Examples: hitting your funny bone (elbow) against a table sends electric shocks up the arm, numbness and tingling when your foot or hand "fall asleep"
