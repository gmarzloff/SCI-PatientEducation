Type of Injury
Paraplegia and Tetraplegia

Paraplegia and tetraplegia are categories of injuries based on how many limbs (arms and legs) are affected. "-plegia" means paralysis. A spinal cord injury will affect the area under the level of the spinal cord injury

Paraplegia

Paraplegia (para=2) is when 2 limbs are affected by paralysis, the legs

Injuries to the thoracic, lumbar, and sacral levels can cause paraplegia

Tetraplegia

Tetraplegia (tetra=four) where four limbs are affected, both arms and legs

Injuries to the cervical levels can cause tetraplegia

Another older term for tetraplegia that can still be heard is Quadriplegia
