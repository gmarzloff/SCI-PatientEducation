Spasticity

The nerves outside of the spinal cord that connect to muscles can still work without signals from the brain. This is called a reflex. A reflex is, for example, what is tested by doctors when the knee is tapped and the leg kicks - no signals are sent to the brain, only from the muscle to the spinal cord and back. In those with spinal cord injury, reflexes can be overactive, and muscles can be very jumpy or "spastic," moving on their own.

Spasticity can be present in those with UMN injuries in their arm and leg muscles, but also in the muscles of their internal organs, like the bladder and bowel.
